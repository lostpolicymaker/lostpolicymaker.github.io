Side events have a longstanding tradition at Hacker Summer Camp. Some, like [Queercon](https://queercon.org), have become destinations on their own, and [Security BSides](http://www.securitybsides.com) spawned a series of events around the world. By and large, these events are run by passionate volunteers, many have formed formal non-profit corporations, and all serve the community from which they came. The official [DEF CON 34 Parties, Meetups, and Events page](https://defcon.org/html/defcon-34/dc-34-pmne.html) and [Communities page](https://defcon.org/html/defcon-34/dc-34-communities.html) are the best places to find current details on locations, times, and agendas.

### Queercon

[Queercon](https://queercon.org), a non-profit with thousands of members, is the largest LGBTQIA+ security group in the world. What started as a hacker party inside DEF CON has grown into a worldwide social network of LGBTQIA+ hackers, and its mixers at DEF CON — designed for LGBTQIA+ individuals and allies to meet, network, and engage to a backdrop of music and refreshments — remain a fixture of the week.

### The Diana Initiative

The [Diana Initiative](https://dianainitiative.org), founded in 2015, focuses on increasing representation of women, gender non-conforming, and non-binary people in the information security community. During Hacker Summer Camp they host TDI @ DEF CON activities, meetups, and a Quiet Room — a library-vibes space where anyone can calm down and recharge. Check [their events page](https://www.dianainitiative.org/events) for this year's schedule.

### Lawyer Meetup AKA the DEF CON Bar Association

It may surprise you, but some lawyers are also hackers (and vice-versa) and have been attending DEF CON for a long time. Attending lawyers practice in varying capacities: private practice, public non-profits, government, and solo-practitioners. Each year the lawyers organize to catch up, talk shop, and network. All lawyers or legal professionals are welcome to join — check the [DEF CON 34 meetups page](https://defcon.org/html/defcon-34/dc-34-pmne.html) for time and location.

### DEF CON Shoot

Many DEF CON attendees with military and law enforcement backgrounds may enjoy [DEF CON Shoot](https://deviating.net/firearms/defcon_shoot/), the long-running pop-up shooting range held just before the conference.

### Friends of Bill W.

Friends of Bill W. meetings are held daily during DEF CON for those in recovery. Times and locations are listed on the [DEF CON 34 meetups page](https://defcon.org/html/defcon-34/dc-34-pmne.html).

### DEAF CON

DEAF CON is a non-profit organization that encourages deaf and hard of hearing individuals within the information security and hacker communities to attend conferences. They have been facilitating communication and providing a platform for networking with others since 2011. Check the [DEF CON 34 Communities page](https://defcon.org/html/defcon-34/dc-34-communities.html) for their current presence.
